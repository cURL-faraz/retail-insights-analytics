# Executive Summary

This analysis shows that **Electronics is the primary driver of business performance**, generating the highest revenue because it combines the largest order volume with the highest average transaction value. **West is the strongest-performing region**, but its advantage is driven by serving more customers and processing more orders rather than higher-value purchases, suggesting that customer acquisition is a stronger growth lever than increasing basket size. Customer satisfaction increases consistently with larger discounts, and this trend remains visible even within major business segments such as Electronics and the West region, indicating that discounting does not appear to harm the customer experience. Revenue is also highly concentrated: a small number of electronic products generate most sales, while the top 10% of customers contribute approximately one-third of total revenue, making both key products and VIP customer retention strategically important. Finally, the sharp sales recovery observed in **June 2024** was driven almost entirely by a surge in Electronics orders rather than balanced growth across all categories. Overall, the findings suggest prioritizing investment in the Electronics category, retaining high-value customers, protecting best-selling products, and expanding successful regional sales strategies to sustain future growth.
